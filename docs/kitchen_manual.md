# Kitchen Staff Manual

Welcome to the **NexusPOS AI Kitchen Display System (KDS)**. The KDS replaces paper tickets, ensuring orders are organized, timed, and never lost.

---

## 1. Accessing the KDS

1. Log in to your tablet or terminal using your provided kitchen credentials.
2. The KDS interface will load automatically.
3. If your kitchen has multiple stations (e.g., Hot Kitchen, Salad Bar, Dessert), use the dropdown in the top right corner to filter tickets so you only see what you are responsible for preparing.

## 2. Understanding Ticket Statuses

When a cashier submits an order, it appears on your screen instantly as a "Ticket." Each ticket has a status:

*   **NEW / PENDING**: The order just arrived. It is waiting to be acknowledged.
*   **PREPARING**: You have acknowledged the order and are actively cooking it.
*   **READY**: The food is plated and waiting for a server or customer to pick it up.
*   **SERVED**: The food has left the kitchen.

### Ticket Timers
Pay close attention to the timer at the top of every ticket. It changes color based on how long the customer has been waiting:
*   🟢 **Green**: Under 5 minutes (On time).
*   🟡 **Yellow**: 5 - 10 minutes (Approaching delay).
*   🔴 **Red**: Over 10 minutes (Priority Action Required).

## 3. Workflow: Processing an Order

### Step 1: Accepting Orders
When a new ticket appears, tap anywhere on the ticket card. The status will instantly change to **PREPARING**. This alerts the front counter that the kitchen has seen the order.

### Step 2: Preparing Orders
Read the ticket carefully.
*   Look for the **Order Type** (Dine-In, Takeaway, Delivery).
*   Look for the **Table Number**.
*   Pay special attention to **Yellow highlighted text**. These are special requests or allergy notes from the cashier (e.g., "No Peanuts").

### Step 3: Marking Orders Ready
Once the food is cooked, plated, and placed on the pass, tap the ticket again. The status will change to **READY**.

### Step 4: Bumping Completed Tickets
When the waiter takes the food to the table, or the customer picks up their takeaway bag, tap the ticket one last time.
The status changes to **SERVED** and the ticket is "bumped" (removed) from your active board.

---

## 4. Troubleshooting & Best Practices

*   **Audio Alerts**: Ensure the volume on your KDS tablet is turned up. A chime will play whenever a new order arrives from the POS.
*   **Accidental Bumps**: If you accidentally bump a ticket to SERVED before it was actually picked up, tell the cashier immediately so they can reprint a physical ticket for you. (A recall feature is coming in a future update).
*   **Dirty Screens**: Keep a dry microfiber cloth nearby. If the tablet gets greasy or wet, it may register "phantom touches" or fail to respond when you tap tickets.
